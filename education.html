Eye Care Tips 👁️

Guidelines for prevention and education of Computer Vision Syndrome (CVS).

What is Computer Vision Syndrome (CVS)?

CVS or Digital Eye Strain is a group of eye and vision problems related to prolonged use of computers, tablets, e-readers, and mobile phones. Forcing the eye muscles to focus at a close distance (< 62 cm) continuously triggers accommodative stress on the ciliary muscles of the eyes, which if left unchecked can lead to long-term deterioration of eye health.

Identification & Treatment

⚠️

Main Causes

  • Screen distance is too close.
  • Poor room lighting (glare).
  • Slouched sitting posture.
  • Blinking frequency drops drastically when focusing.
🩺

Common Symptoms

  • Eyes feel dry, sore, or itchy.
  • Blurred or double vision.
  • Tension headache.
  • Stiff pain in the neck, shoulders, and upper back.
🩹

First Aid

  • Immediately stop staring at digital screens.
  • Close your eyes gently for 1-2 minutes for relaxation.
  • Use eye drops (artificial tears) if dry.
  • Perform light neck stretches.

Active Prevention Steps

⏱️

20-20-20 Rule

Every 20 minutes of work, look at an object 20 feet (6 meters) away for 20 seconds to reset the focus muscles of your eyes.

📏

Screen Distance & Position

Ensure the distance from your eyes to the screen remains above the safe limit of 62 cm. Position the center of the screen about 15-20 degrees below eye level.

💡

Lighting Optimization

Use proper room lighting of 300-500 Lux. Position your monitor to avoid external light reflections (glare).
